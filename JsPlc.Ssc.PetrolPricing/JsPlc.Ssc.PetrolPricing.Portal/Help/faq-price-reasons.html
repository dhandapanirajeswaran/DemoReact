Based on Unleaded

This means the price is based on the price plus an additional markup value (e.g. +5.0p)

The Super-Unleaded Markup can be found on the Settings page.

Cheapest price Found

This means that the cheapest competitor has been found (there is some competitor price) and is based on the following:-

  1. There are competitors within 25 minute drive time
  2. Competitor Price Data is available
  3. Drive-Time markup is included when finding the cheapest competitor

Nearby Grocers

Within the surrounding Competitors it is possible that there are one or more Grocers (such as ASDA, Morrisons or even Sainsbury's). The range is 25 minutes

You can manage the Grocers on the Brands & Grocers page.

TESCO is grouped in many reports with TESCO EXPRESS and TESCO EXTRA

Incomplete Grocers

When nearby Grocers exist a check is made to deterine if we have up-to-date price data. When is this not the case, the price will Snapback to the Today's price and the price reason will include "Has Incomplete Grocers"

Inside Price-Variance

Whenever a Price Move is within a small variance range it is uneconomical for Sainsburys to ask the Fuel Site Collegue to change the forecourt prices. In this case the Today price will be used for the Tomorrow price — a Price Snapback occurs.

The Price Change Variance setting can be found on the Settings page

Latest JS Price

The Price has been sourced from the Latest JS Price Data file upload.

Manual Price Override

A price marked as Override or Manual Price Override means a user has entered a new price (an 'Override') to replace the suggested price based on the surrounding competitors.

Price Overrides are entered on the main Pricing page for each site and each Fuel combination.

Match Competitor Price Found

When the Match Competitor option has been used for a Site as the Price Match Strategy then the Match Competitor site is used as the basis of the price.

The normal Drive Time markup is not applied to the matched price.

Missing Daily Catalist

This means that there is no Daily Catalist price file uploaded today.

You should always upload the latest Catalist price file each day before pricing the Sainsburys fuel prices.

Missing Site CatNo

Sainsburys sites are found using the CatNo (Catalist number) which is unique per site. If within the Petrol Pricing tool a site does not have this number then it cannot price.

No Match-Competitor Price

This means that the sites which has been used for the Match Competitor Price Match Strategy does not have a fuel price.

No Suggested Price

The Suggested price is also called the Auto price and will become the Tomorrow price for each Site Fuel combination unless a user enters a Price Override

This means a price cannot be calculated usually when the Petrol Pricing tool is first setup and there is simply not enough price data to find the cheapest competitor price.

Outside Price-Variance

This is the opposite of the Inside Price Variance reason.

A fuel price change (or 'price movement') exceeds the Price Change Variance threshold. This means that the price change will be sent out to Sainsburys sites when the email is sent.

Price Stunt Freeze

This means that a Price Freeze (also known as a Price Stunt) has/is taking place so price cannot legally increase during this time period. Prices can descrease but cannot increase.

Often the Price Reason will also include the Price Snapback reason.

Rounded

This means that Rounding (or more correctly replacement of the last digit in a price) has taken place.

For example a price of 114.4 will be "rounded" to 114.9 (assuming a rounding of 9).

The Decimal Rounding value can be found on the Settings page.

Today Price SnapBack

A Price Snapback (or Snapback for short) means that the cheapest competitor/suggested price has been ignored and the previous Today Price will be used for Tomorrow's price.

The Price Snapback will occur in a number of suituations

  1. A Price Freeze has stopped the price from increasing
  2. There is Incomplete Grocer Data

Trial Price Found

Similiar to the Match Competitor option this Price Match Stategy affect the Suggested (aka Auto Price). The Price is based on the cheapest Competitor price plus an additional markup value.

The Trial price (+/-) markup value is set on a site-by-site basis.
