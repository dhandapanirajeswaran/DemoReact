Glossary of terms

Here is a list of the commonly used terms within the Petrol Pricing tool

Bulk Or Mass Override
A Bulk or Mass Override is when multiple petrol sites have one of the Fuel Types prices set. (see the Site Pricing page)
Competitor
A competitor is either a Saninsburys or competitor (such as ASDA, TESCO or an independant petrol retailer) site. — Competitor are searched based on Drive Time distance from each individual Sainsburys Fuel site.
Decimal Rounding
This is when the last digit of a Modal Price is replaced with a different digit. For example you may wish to change all prices to end with .9 — This can be done on the Settings page
Drive Time
This is the amount of time it takes to reach a Competitor fuel site. When calculating prices the Drive Time is taken into consideration and a Drive Time Markup is added to the base Fuel price.
Drive Time Markup
This is a Markup added to the base Fuel price based on the amount of DriveTime — The markup values are managed on the Drive Time markup settings page.
Fuel Types
The Petrol Pricing tool supported 3 fuel Types — Unleaded, Diesel and Super-Unleaded
Fuel Markup
This is an additional cost (markup) applied to the base price of a Fuel
(E.g. Super-Unleaded could be +5.0p more expensive than Unleaded)
Grocer
A Grocer is a large scale (large grocing) Competitor Brand (such as ASDA or TESCO)
Manual Override
A Manual Override is the same as a Override
Modal Price
This is the actual price multipled by 10.
E.g. 123.4 will be stored as the Modal price of 1234
Nearby Grocer
A Nearby Grocer is a Grocer within a small Drive Time (such as 10 minutes)
Price Variance
This is a threshold value used when deciding whether it is economical to send a fuel price change out to a site, or, to Price Snapback to the Today Price
Price Freeze
When a Price Freeze happens we cannot for legal reasons increase our fuel prices for a period of time (E.g. 7 days). — However, we can lower the prices of our fuels. Price Freeze Events can be managed on the Price Freeze Event page
Price Snapback
This is when a Suggested Price is rejected and the Today Price is used.
Override
An Override is a way for a user to ignore (or Override) the Suggested Price and specify a different price
Super-Unleaded
The Super-Unleaded fuel price is often based on the price of Unleaded plus a Fuel Markup cost.
The Super-Unleaded Markup can found on the Settings page
Today
This means the Viewing Date on the main pricing page
Tomorrow
This is the Suggested Price or Override Price for Tomorrow

These are the prices which will be sent via email to the individual Sainsburys Petrol sites.
