Price Freeze

During a Price Freeze we cannot legally increase the price of any of our fuels on any site.

We can make further price decreases during the Price Freeze

A banner appears at the top of the Pricing Page indicating that a Price Freeze is active and how many days are remaining.

On the last day of a you continue pricing as normal (price increases are allowed) for Tomorrow

Competitor Modal

The Competitor Modal can be access on the Pricing page by clicking on a Sainsbury's Store Name. Its purpose is to show which competitor was chosen (if any) for each fuel type.

The modal itself shows all the surrounding Competitors (including other Sainsburys sites) with (for example) 25 minutes Drive Time. Each of the 3 Fuel grades (Unleaded, Diesel, and Super-Unleaded) are displayed with their prices for the previous two days (Day -1 and Day -2).

For each fuel type the chosen Cheapest Competitor price is highlighted.

For each fuel type the age of the Fuel price is displayed (e.g. 3d = 3 days or 'X' no price available).

The Suggested Price will also include any Drive Time Markup and possibly any Trial Price Markup or Match Competitor markup.

Nearby Grocers

A Nearby Grocer is any Grocer within (for example) 10 minute Drive Time

A Nearby Grocer is a Competitor within X minutes.

You can change the X minutes drive time threshold in the Settings page using the Nearby Grocer Drive Time field.

Bulk Override

A Bulk (aka Mass) Override is entered on the Pricing Page under each of the 3 Fuel signs and will set the Override Price for a single fuel for ALL sites

— —

There are two modes of entering a Bulk Override, these are...

(+/-n)
This is a small increase/decrease value (E.g. -2.0 will move all the Prices down by 2.0p)
This will set the Absolute Price for all the sites (E.g. 109.0 will set all the sites to the price £109.00)

Today And Tomorrow Prices

Today prices are the prices which are currently displayed at each Sainsburys fuel site

Tomorrow prices are the Suggested (aka Auto) price which have been calculated by looking at all the surrounding Competitor fuel prices for that particular site. Drive Time markup is applied when finding the cheapest price and is based on the Drive Time markup values.

Override prices are user entered prices and will be used instead of the Suggested/Auto price.

Either the Override or Suggested Tomorrow price will be sent out to each Sainsburys fuel site via email.

Price Match Type (aka Site Price Strategy)

This is the strategy of how a Sainsburys Suggested Price is calculated from surrounding Competitors (normally within 25 minutes drive time).

There are currently 3 types of Price Match Types

Standard
The Standard takes the cheapest Competitor price for each Fuel Type — Drive Time Markup is added to this base price.
Trial Price
The Trial Price takes the cheapest Competitor price for each Fuel Type and then applies an additional Markup (E.g. +2.0p) — Drive Time Markup is added to this base price.
Match Competitor
The Match Competitor uses a one, fixed Competitor to use as its base price (other Competitors are not used) then an additional markup is applied.

Match Competitor does not apply Drive Time Markup based on the amount of Drive Time/Distance from the Sainsburys site to the Matched Competitor— It is up to the user to specific the total markup in the Match Competitor +/- value.

Site Emails

For each Sainsburys site fuel price change an email will be sent out to that fuel site only if there is a price change for any of the 3 fuel types.

It is possible that very small changes in fuel prices (such as +0.1p or -0.7p) might be ignored depending on the Price Change Variance value. If the change is within this Price Variance then the price is Snapped back to the Today Price

Suggested Price

This is the Suggested (aka the Auto) Price and is used when calculating the Tomorrow Price.

The price itself is built on some complex pricing logic which is based on surrounding Competitors, their Drive Time distance and the Fuel Type

Absolute Min/Max Prices

You can only enter Fuel Prices between a min and max range. This helps to prevent user errors when entering Override Prices.

You cannot enter a value smaller than the Min value or higher than the Max limit.

The Absolute Min/Max Prices are managed on the Settings page for each Fuel Type.

Warning Indicators

When entering a Bulk Override the UI will warning you if you are entering a price change which it outside a certain range. You are still free to make the price change.

You can review and manage the Warning Indicator limits in the Settings page.
